Employment Background & Education

I have a pretty classic background. I grew up in Eldon, MO and graduated high school in 2012. After graduation I attended the local community college to get the "cheap" general education requirements out of the way before moving to Springfield in 2014 to attend Missouri State University.

After dipping my toes in a few different programs ranging from Education to Dietetics, I finally settled somewhere in the middle. I graduated from MSU in 2017 with a BA in Professional Writing. This degree program offered practical business knowledge and writing skills in order for any graduate to land a job in whatever business they desire.

Central Bank of the Ozarks

2014 - 2017


Through college I worked at a local Springfield bank. I absolutely loved the work, process, and people. I believe this is where I learned "process" and how effective it can be when followed. I mean, you have to follow process at a bank in order to keep your job and perform. However, it gave me a lot of organizational skills I still have today.






Trinity Health Care, Prime Inc.

2016 - 2017


The Professional Writing degree program requires its students to find an internship at a local business and fulfil a documentation need they may have.


I chose Trinity Healthcare, which is the internal health practice within Prime, Inc. I was tasked with documenting their new EMS (electronic medical system) and to create user, system, and how-to guids for the health care employees used the system every day.



Digital Monitoring Products (DMP)

2017 - 2018


I started at DMP as a technical writer immediately after graduation. (for anyone still in school and about to land a full-time gig, do yourself a favor and take the break! You deserve it.)


DMP manufactures security panels and products. My responsibilities incuded writing all the hardware documentation for thos products. The documentation list is a mile long, but a few of those docs included Installation Guides, User Guides, Complience Guides, and How-To Guides.


O'Reilly Auto Parts

2018 - Present


I was looking for more of a challenge in my career. I had friends who raved about O'Reilly culture and how fun the systems were to work on. I had no idea at the time that I'd learn to appreciate software and what goes into building it.


I made a lateral move from DMP and started as a technical writer. In a few short years with just as many projects under my belt, I was offered the analyst position and still in that role today.
